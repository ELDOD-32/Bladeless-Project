About the Problem

Egypt faces rising energy demands and environmental pollution due to heavy reliance on fossil fuels. Traditional wind turbines are noisy, costly, and not suited for urban or wildlife-sensitive areas.

Our Solution

We developed a bladeless wind turbine that harnesses wind using vortex-induced vibrations (VIV). The motion powers a slider-crank and rack-pinion system connected to a DC motor, producing 50.3J in 5 minutes. It is compact, bird-safe, quiet, and low-cost.

Prototype & Testing

Dimensions: 1.05m height, 0.4m × 0.2m base. The system showed great performance in trials with consistent oscillation and energy generation. Testing involved voltage/current tracking and confirmed the design exceeded the 40J target.
